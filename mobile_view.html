Frequently Asked Questions

Scammers use various sophisticated techniques on social media platforms to deceive users and steal personal information or money.

  • Creating fake profiles that look legitimate
  • Using emotional manipulation and urgency
  • Offering too-good-to-be-true deals
  • Phishing through direct messages
  • Romance scams targeting lonely individuals

This is really scary - someone might copy your profile photo and name to create a fake account that looks exactly like you! Then they'll message your friends asking for money or make posts pretending to be you.

Signs of Impersonation

  • Getting notifications about logins from unknown devices or cities
  • Your friends and family telling you about strange messages "from you"
  • Finding another account using your name and photos
  • Posts or activities appearing that you never did

What to Do If It Happens

  • Take screenshots immediately as proof. Don't try messaging the fake account directly
  • Report the fake account to Facebook/Instagram/WhatsApp, and change all your passwords immediately
  • Warn all your friends and family about the imposter
  • Search your name online once in a while to catch imposters early
  • Enable two-factor authentication on every account possible

Learn to identify suspicious messages that could be scams or phishing attempts targeting your personal information.

  • Messages claiming you've won prizes or lotteries
  • Requests for OTP or verification codes
  • Links asking for personal information
  • Messages with urgent threats or deadlines
  • Forwards with unverified health or news claims

Fake giveaways and contests are common scam tactics used to collect personal information or spread malware.

  • Check the legitimacy of the organizing company
  • Look for official verification badges
  • Be wary of contests requiring upfront payments
  • Verify contest rules and terms
  • Check if the prize value seems realistic

Fraudulent job offers can lead to identity theft, financial loss, or becoming unknowingly involved in illegal activities.

  • Unrealistic salary promises for simple work
  • Requests for upfront payments or fees
  • Poor grammar and spelling in communications
  • Vague job descriptions
  • Immediate hiring without proper interviews

If you accidentally click a suspicious link, take immediate action to protect your device and personal information.

  • Close the browser tab immediately
  • Run a full antivirus scan
  • Change passwords for all important accounts
  • Check bank and credit card statements
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Monitor accounts for unusual activity

Malicious applications can steal your data, spy on your activities, or damage your device.

  • Only download apps from official app stores
  • Check app permissions carefully
  • Read user reviews and ratings
  • Verify the developer's authenticity
  • Avoid apps requesting unnecessary permissions
  • Never download APK files from unknown sources

Fake shopping websites can steal your payment information and personal details without delivering any products.

  • Check for HTTPS security certificate
  • Look for contact information and physical address
  • Read customer reviews and ratings
  • Verify payment security measures
  • Check return and refund policies
  • Be wary of prices that seem too good to be true
