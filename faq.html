Learn to identify suspicious messages that could be scams or phishing attempts targeting your personal information.

  • Messages claiming you've won prizes or lotteries you never entered
  • Requests for OTP or verification codes from "banks"
  • Links asking for personal information or account details
  • Messages with urgent threats or unrealistic deadlines
  • Forwards with unverified health or news claims
  • Messages from unknown numbers claiming to be relatives

Fake giveaways and contests are common scam tactics used to collect personal information or spread malware.

Red Flags

  • No official verification badges on company profiles
  • Contests requiring upfront payments or fees
  • Prizes that seem unreasonably valuable
  • No clear terms and conditions

How to Verify

  • Check the company's official website and social media
  • Look for contest announcements on verified accounts
  • Verify contest rules and eligibility criteria
  • Research the company's past giveaway history

Fraudulent job offers can lead to identity theft, financial loss, or becoming unknowingly involved in illegal activities.

  • Unrealistic salary promises for simple or vague work
  • Requests for upfront payments or registration fees
  • Poor grammar and spelling in official communications
  • Vague job descriptions with no specific requirements
  • Immediate hiring without proper interviews or background checks
  • Requests for personal documents before job confirmation

If you accidentally click a suspicious link, take immediate action to protect your device and personal information.

  • Close the browser tab immediately without entering any information
  • Run a full antivirus scan on your device
  • Change passwords for all important accounts
  • Check bank and credit card statements for unusual activity
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Monitor accounts closely for any unauthorized access
  • Report the suspicious link to relevant authorities

Malicious applications can steal your data, spy on your activities, or damage your device.

  • Only download apps from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store)
  • Check app permissions carefully before installing
  • Read user reviews and ratings thoroughly
  • Verify the developer's authenticity and reputation
  • Avoid apps requesting unnecessary permissions
  • Never download APK files from unknown or untrusted sources
  • Be wary of apps promising unrealistic features

Fake shopping websites can steal your payment information and personal details without delivering any products.

What to Check

  • HTTPS security certificate (padlock icon)
  • Complete contact information and physical address
  • Customer reviews and ratings from multiple sources
  • Clear return and refund policies

Warning Signs

  • Prices that seem too good to be true
  • Poor website design and grammar errors
  • Limited payment options or suspicious methods
  • No customer service contact information

Banking fraud is one of the most serious forms of online scams. Learn how to keep your financial information safe.

  • Never share your ATM PIN, CVV, or OTP with anyone
  • Always type your bank's URL directly, never click links from emails
  • Use only official banking apps from app stores
  • Enable SMS and email alerts for all transactions
  • Regularly check bank statements for unauthorized transactions
  • Use separate cards for online and offline transactions
  • Never use public WiFi for banking transactions

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against cybercriminals. Learn to create and manage them effectively.

Creating Strong Passwords

  • Use at least 12 characters with mixed case, numbers, symbols
  • Avoid personal information like birthdays or names
  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Consider using passphrases instead of passwords

Password Management

  • Use a reputable password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication everywhere
  • Regularly update passwords for important accounts
  • Never share passwords with others
